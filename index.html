What is it?

Graphic Design is the practice of composing and communicating ideas visually

(We design digital & analogue things for people to see)

We solve problems!

You will explore these relationships through creative play & experimentation to help you understand and develop your visual communication.

You will investigate Graphic Design in the broadest sense, considering it in relation to 4 modules:


  • Contextual Studies (Theory of Everything)
  • Graphic Communication (You and the client)
  • Visual Expression (Your own creativity)
  • Design Fundamentals (Skills and Industry links)

All within a creative, design and technology ethos.

What does that work consist of?

The work demands creative flair, lateral thinking, an enquiring mind, up-to-date knowledge of industry software and a professional approach to time, costs and deadlines.

  • Ideation
  • Research
  • Experimentation
  • Development
  • Refinement

It requires logical and creative thinking

We embrace traditional and emerging areas of design practice.

"Today, designers are expected to be multi-specialised in both physical and digital spaces, and the future is a blend between Artificial Inteligence (AI), Augmented Reality (AR), VirtualReality (VR), Mixed Reality (MR) and "In real life" (IRL) to create fully immersive brand experiences"- Holly Karlsson, creative director at Bulletproof.
